# MoodMuse — Presentation Script

---

## SLIDE 1 — TITLE

**MoodMuse**

A Museum of Feelings

*Turn a feeling into something you can hold.*

---

## SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM

Have you ever wanted to make something — but didn't know what?

Not because you had no materials.
Not because you had no time.

Because you had no *reason*.

That blank moment kills more creativity than anything else.

---

## SLIDE 3 — WHAT WE BUILT

MoodMuse is a personal museum that grows every time you feel something.

You tell it how you feel.
You show it what's nearby.
It gives you something meaningful to make.

Then it keeps what you made — forever — as a collectible artifact in a magical museum that is only yours.

---

## SLIDE 4 — HOW IT WORKS

**Step 1 — Name the feeling**
Pick a mood. Nostalgic. Inspired. Healing. Curious.

**Step 2 — Show what you have**
Upload a photo of whatever is within arm's reach. Canvas, markers, pencils — the app sees them.

**Step 3 — Receive your project**
Not a generic craft tutorial. A specific, emotionally meaningful project designed around your mood and your exact materials. Under 30 minutes.

---

## SLIDE 5 — THE MAGIC

Before you've even started making it, your project already has a name.

*"The Stars She Took With Her"*

It has a museum room.

*Hall of Nostalgia*

It has a plaque.

*"A handmade constellation of recent memories, showing the creator surrounded by the moments, places, and dreams that quietly shaped her next chapter."*

Your craft just became an artifact.

---

## SLIDE 6 — THE PROJECT

For our demo, the mood was *Nostalgic* and the materials were *canvas, markers, pencils, and brushes*.

MoodMuse generated this:

**"The Girl Who Carried Her Chapters"**

Draw yourself in the center of a canvas. Surround yourself with small symbols of recent meaningful events — a plane for travel, a laptop for work, a heart for people you miss, a door for a new chapter. Connect the symbols like constellations. Add color to show which memories feel warm, painful, exciting, or unfinished.

20 minutes. Gentle difficulty. Five clear steps.

Not a tutorial. A ritual.

## SLIDE 11 — DESIGN

---

## SLIDE 12 — TECH

Built in 24 hours.

**Frontend:** Single-page HTML/CSS/JS inside a Next.js 15 shell. Mobile-first responsive. Runs on any device.

**Backend:** Next.js API routes. TypeScript. In-memory store for the hackathon. Supabase-ready for production.

**AI-ready:** Pluggable vision + generation (Gemini, GPT-4o, or Claude). Material detection from photos. Emotionally-tuned project generation. All working behind a mock mode for the demo.

**Museum UI:** CSS-only celestial theater. Velvet curtains, gold shelves, tarot-style artifact cards, storybook detail modal. No Three.js. No WebGL. Pure CSS magic.
